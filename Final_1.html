You would like your child to become a dual citizen of the United States and Mexico.

 

We can help! Both U.S. and Mexican law permit dual citizenship. U.S. citizens who become citizens of other countries do not lose their U.S. citizenship unless they renounce it. However, it is important to note that Mexican citizens may only hold passports of two countries. So, if your child becomes a dual citizen of the United States and Mexico, he or she cannot carry a passport of a third country without violating Mexican law.

 

Because your child is already a U.S. citizen and carries a U.S. passport, he/she only needs to apply for Mexican citizenship and a Mexican passport. Here are the following steps you need to take:

 

1.     Obtain a Mexican Birth Certificate for Your Child

 

The Mexican birth certificate is required to apply for your child’s Mexican nationality and passport. To get the birth certificate, you must present the following documents to your Mexican consulate:

 

·      Child’s U.S. birth certificate (original and copy)

·      Child’s government ID (drivers license or ID card)

·      Mexican parents’ birth certificate or valid Mexican passport (originals and copies)

·      Copy of Mexican parent’s official valid ID card or drivers license

·      Copy of two (2) witnesses’ drivers license or ID card

·      Two passport photographs of child

 

Remember to copy both sides of the documents if they contain any information (especially drivers licenses and ID cards). If you are missing any of the required documents, click here to see where you can obtain them.

 

Some consulates require that you make an appointment. Click here to find your nearest consulate and learn about their specific procedures.

 

2.     Apply for Citizenship

 

To apply for Mexican citizenship, you must present the following documents to a Mexican consulate:

·      DNN-1 Application (original and copy)

o   Available in print at Mexican Consulates

·      Child’s Mexican birth certificate issued by Consulate (certified copy)

·      Older siblings’ Mexican birth certificate, issued by Mexican Civil Registry (certified copy)

·      Mexican parent’s birth certificate, issued by Mexican Civil Registry office (certified copy)

·      Parents’ marriage certificate, issued by Mexican Civil Registry office (certified copy)

·      Documentation from immigration authorities that shows date of admission to foreign country

3.     Apply for Mexican Passport

 

The Mexican government requires the parents’ consent to issue passports to children under the age of 18. Because you and your child’s mother/father are married, only one of you must appear at consulate with your child.

 

To apply for a Mexican passport for your child, you must present the following items to a Mexican consulate:

:

·      OP-5 Application Form (original and copy)

o   Available to download online at http://sre.gob.mx/formatos-de-solicitud

o   Available in print at Mexican consulates

·      Child’s Mexican birth certificate

·      Two passport photos

·      One of the following documents:

o   Parents’ marriage certificate, issued by Mexican Civil Registry office (certified copy)

o   Older siblings’ Mexican birth certificate, issued by Mexican Civil Registry (certified copy)

o   Documentation from immigration authorities that shows date of admission to foreign country
